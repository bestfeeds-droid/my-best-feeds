Our Thesis
Maritime trade no longer reacts only to danger itself.
It reacts to the anticipation of danger.
The instrument serving that world must be built at the same speed.
Today, a vessel does not need to enter a conflict zone for its voyage economics to unravel. A rerouting instruction, an AIS anomaly near a sensitive corridor, a shift in regional intelligence signals — any of these is now sufficient to trigger underwriting reassessment, charterparty friction, and cargo anxiety simultaneously. The physical event has become secondary. The anticipation of it arrives first.

That gap — between when the market reacts and when insurance responds — is where commercial voyages are haemorrhaging value. And it is the gap that NANOFINS was built to close.

The Instrument
A next-generation marine insurance instrument calibrated to the signals modern maritime risk actually moves on — corridor-specific, independently verified, and structured to respond at the speed of the market. Fast settlement. No lag between event and compensation.
02
The Corridor
Gulf of Guinea via Cape of Good Hope — anchored at Lekki Deep Sea Port, Africa's deepest at 16.5m draft. The primary commercially viable alternative as Hormuz and Red Sea remain compromised.
03
The Capital
Sovereign-grade multilateral capital backstop — structured to replace London's reinsurance function on this corridor. Premium retained in Lagos. The margin that has always left Nigeria stays here.
Commercial Architecture
The architecture connects vessel operators rerouting through the Gulf of Guinea corridor, a Nigerian anchor insurer retaining marine premium domestically, and a multilateral DFI capital facility providing the sovereign-grade backstop that makes the product commercially executable at scale — into a single, structured risk instrument. When it responds, it responds fast. No assessor. No negotiation. No margin leaving Nigeria.
